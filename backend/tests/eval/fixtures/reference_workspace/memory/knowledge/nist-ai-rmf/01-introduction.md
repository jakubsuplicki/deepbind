# NIST AI RMF — Introduction

## Purpose

The NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) was developed in response to
direction from the National AI Initiative Act of 2020. It is intended to help
organizations manage AI risks in a way that:

- Improves the ability to incorporate trustworthiness into AI design, development,
  use, and evaluation.
- Provides a common language and organizational framework.
- Applies to all stages of the AI lifecycle.

The framework is **voluntary**. It is designed to be used by any organization
involved with AI — regardless of size, sector, or the AI systems they deploy.

## What "Trustworthy AI" Means in the NIST RMF

The NIST AI RMF defines trustworthy AI systems as those that are:

1. **Valid and Reliable** — outputs are consistently accurate and the system
   behaves as expected across conditions.
2. **Safe** — the system avoids undue harm to human life, health, property, or
   the environment.
3. **Secure and Resilient** — the system resists adversarial manipulation and
   recovers from failures.
4. **Explainable and Interpretable** — stakeholders can understand why the system
   makes particular decisions.
5. **Privacy-Enhanced** — the system respects individual privacy preferences and
   handles personal data appropriately.
6. **Fair, with Bias Managed** — the system does not create or reinforce unfair
   discrimination.
7. **Accountable and Transparent** — responsibilities are clear and AI-related
   decisions can be audited.

## Relationship with Other Frameworks

The NIST AI RMF explicitly acknowledges that it does not exist in isolation:
- It complements the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)** for AI-specific risks
  in security (particularly relevant to data poisoning and adversarial attacks
  covered in OWASP LLM Top 10).
- It aligns with ISO/IEC 42001 and IEEE 7000 series.
- It was cited in the US Executive Order on AI (October 2023) as the preferred
  risk management approach for federal AI acquisitions.

## The Four Core Functions

The framework is built around four functions: **Govern, Map, Measure, Manage**.
These are not sequential phases but interdependent capabilities that AI-deploying
organizations should develop in parallel.
