# Executive Summary

The 2025 AI Index documents AI's accelerating trajectory across research, deployment,
and governance. Key findings spanning the last twelve months:

## Research Output

- Global peer-reviewed AI publications rose 24% year-over-year, with China producing
  the largest volume (38% of total) and the United States achieving the highest
  citation impact.
- Open-source AI model releases doubled compared to 2023, accounting for 48% of all
  significant model releases tracked in 2024.

## Technical Milestones

- AI systems for the first time exceeded average human performance on the MMLU
  benchmark (89.0 AI vs 88.6 human), raising concerns about benchmark saturation.
- Multimodal models (image + text) reached mainstream deployment; GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5,
  and Claude 3 each achieved state-of-the-art results on multiple simultaneous tasks.

## Compute and Investment

- Training compute for frontier models grew approximately **2–3× between 2023 and
  2024**, continuing the decade-long trend despite some signals of slowing.
- Total global AI private investment reached $252 billion in 2024, up 26% from 2023,
  with generative AI capturing ~$67 billion (27% of the total).

## Responsibility and Safety

- Red-teaming and pre-deployment safety evaluations became standard practice at major
  labs, though no common evaluation framework has yet been adopted industry-wide.
- Government AI incident databases tracked 233 notable AI-related harms in 2024,
  up from 123 in 2022.

## Workforce and Economy

- Demand for AI-related skills in job postings increased 35% globally; supply-side
  graduation rates lag in most countries outside the United States and China.
- Workers in repetitive cognitive roles report the highest disruption from generative
  AI, while roles requiring physical dexterity saw the least impact.
