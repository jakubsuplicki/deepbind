# Policy and Governance

## Global Regulatory Landscape

2024 saw the most significant growth in AI regulation since the field's inception:

- **EU AI Act** entered into force. High-risk AI systems (medical, judicial, legal)
  face conformity assessments and registration. Prohibited uses (social scoring,
  certain real-time biometrics) take effect first; general-purpose AI model rules
  apply from August 2025.
- **United States**: Executive Order 14110 implementation continued; the NIST AI RMF
  was adopted as a voluntary standard across federal agencies.
- **China**: Algorithmic recommendation and generative AI regulations (enacted 2023)
  began active enforcement in 2024.
- **United Kingdom**: Sector-specific oversight model proposed; no single AI Act.
- **India**: Draft AI governance framework published for public comment.

## AI Safety and Incident Tracking

- Government-maintained AI incident databases tracked **233 notable AI-related harms**
  in 2024, compared to 123 in 2022 — an 89% increase, though partly attributable to
  improved reporting mechanisms.
- Categories by frequency: misinformation/deepfakes (29%), bias and discrimination
  (22%), privacy violations (18%), autonomous-system failures (16%), other (15%).

## International Coordination

- The Bletchley Declaration (28 nations) produced no binding commitments but
  established a forum for ongoing safety dialogue.
- The United Nations AI Advisory Body recommended a multi-stakeholder AI governance
  mechanism modelled loosely on the IPCC structure.

## Responsible AI Practices at Organizations

A survey of 500 large organizations found:
- 61% have published an AI ethics/responsible AI policy (up from 44% in 2022).
- 38% have a dedicated AI governance board or committee.
- Only 19% have a mandatory pre-deployment risk assessment process.

Red-teaming before deployment became a common practice among frontier AI labs
(OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta AI) though methodologies remain divergent.
