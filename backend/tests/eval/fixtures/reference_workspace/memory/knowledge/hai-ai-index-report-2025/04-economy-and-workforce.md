# Economy and Workforce

## Private Investment

Global private AI investment reached **$252 billion in 2024**, up 26% from $200B
in 2023. Generative AI captured **$67 billion** (~27% of total), a 2.3× increase
from $29B in 2023.

United States dominated investment at $109B (43% of global), followed by China ($48B)
and the United Kingdom ($20B). India emerged as the fourth-largest AI investment
destination at $8B.

## Labour Market Impact

- Job postings requiring AI skills grew **35%** globally between 2023 and 2024.
- Supply lags: AI/ML graduate completions grew only 12% in the same period.
- **Workers in repetitive cognitive roles** (data entry, customer support, routine
  analysis) report the highest disruption rates (42% reported significant changes
  to their role due to AI tools).
- **Workers in physical dexterity roles** (construction, manufacturing) saw the
  least impact (9%).

## Generative AI Adoption

McKinsey Global Survey (cited by HAI) found that **78% of responding organizations**
in 2024 used generative AI in at least one business function, up from 55% in 2023.

Most common use cases:
1. Customer service and support (48%)
2. Marketing content creation (41%)
3. Software development assistance (38%)
4. Internal knowledge management (32%)
5. Document summarization and analysis (29%)

## AI Startup and Ecosystem

- The number of AI-focused startups globally reached ~8,000 active ventures in 2024.
- Median time from founding to first institutional funding fell from 24 months to
  14 months, reflecting intense investor interest.
- Acquisitions of AI startups by large technology companies totaled 124 in 2024.
